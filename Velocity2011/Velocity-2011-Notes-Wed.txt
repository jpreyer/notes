- Why the Yahoo FrontPage Went Down?
  - Jake Loomis (Yahoo!)
  - 40,000 req/sec on main page at peak!
  - Tip 1 : redundancy!
    - Make everything redundant!!!
    - Everything can and will fail at some point.
    - Page fails completely!  (show static page generated by cron)
  - Tip 2 : Error proffing change
    - continuous integration environment with auto builds, unit test, deploy and test for each check-in
    - auto tests and debug statementd in QA
      - treat QA like prod (same monitoring)
    - Forked copies of prod traffic into staging environment
      - catch new error messages b4 prod
    - Dark launched code
      - Push first, then activate later
    - Be able to recover quickly
  - Tip 3 : Global load balancing
    - route traffic to nearest of a dozen colos worldwide
    - ability to serve any markey from any data center
  - Tip 4 : Monitor EVERYTHING!
    - Tornado -> royal wedding -> Bin Laden in 72 hours (remember the graph!)
  - Tip 5 : Fallback plans in case of failure
    - Isolate failures 
    - Drop features to add capcity (Dark launched code!!!!)
    - Learn from your mistakes, follow up on your learnings
      - Pie chart of causes of outages (change, capacity, preexisting, non-yahoo, hardware, unknown)
  - Bonus Tip : Rockstar Talent
    - folks who understand your systems, code, etc.....
  - At Yahoo, Service engineering (see the slides on job responsibilities)
  - So why did the page so down!!!!!
    - Front page cache (APC cache, a PHP thing) creation crashed machines in 3 different colos!  Other colos could not handle capacity
